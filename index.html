Drug Scheduling

By Carmen Reinicke

The Drug Enforcement Administration classifies drugs, substances, and chemicals used to make drugs into five categories called schedules as outlined by the Controlled Substance Act.

"It's a good, safe effort," said Jonathan Caulkins, a drug policy researcher and professor at Carnegie Mellon. "It is lampooned by people who want to make policies foolish and there's an intentional misunderstanding of schedule 1."

Drug Schedules

Schedule 1
Drugs with NO currently accepted medical use and a high potential for abuse
Schedule 2
Drugs with a high potential for abuse. Can lead to severe psychological or physical dependence
Schedule 3
Drugs with moderate to low potential for abuse, physical and psychological dependence.
Schedule 4
Drugs with low potential for abuse or dependence.
Schedule 5
Drugs with lower potential for abuse than schedule 4.

"The international treaties recognize no benefit of a psychoactive drug other than medicine," Caulkins continued, "So if there's a drug out there that provides fun, joy, with mild addictive liability, it is banned."

This argument is most easily applied to marijuana, which provides fun and relaxation with a low potential for addiction. The substance, however, is ranked schedule 1, along with heroin and cocaine.
